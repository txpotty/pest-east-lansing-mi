Termites are master hiders. They can build colonies within the walls and structure of your home for years without visible signs of damage. Targeted Termite Treatment Methods

Based on the findings of our inspection, we will recommend the most appropriate treatment method. We offer a range of solutions to effectively eliminate termites and protect your property.

Liquid Termite Treatments

Liquid termiticides are a common and effective method for creating a protective barrier around your property. These treatments involve applying a liquid insecticide to the soil around the foundation, creating a treated zone that termites cannot penetrate. Some advanced liquid treatments are non-repellent, meaning termites unknowingly enter the treated area and carry the product back to the colony, leading to colony elimination. This method is particularly effective for subterranean termites.

Termite Baiting Systems

Termite baiting systems offer a discreet yet powerful approach to termite control. Bait stations, typically installed around the perimeter of your property, contain a cellulose material mixed with a slow-acting insecticide. Termites consume the bait and share it with other colony members, including the queen, ultimately leading to the collapse of the entire colony. Baiting systems are an excellent option for ongoing monitoring and control, providing long-term protection.

Preventative Measures and Monitoring

Our termite treatment isn't just about eliminating current infestations; it's also about preventing future ones. We offer recommendations and can implement preventative measures to make your property less attractive to termites.

Reducing Moisture

Termites are attracted to moisture. Addressing plumbing leaks, ensuring proper drainage away from the foundation, and reducing humidity in crawl spaces can significantly reduce the risk of infestation.

Eliminating Wood-to-Soil Contact

Direct contact between wood and soil provides termites with easy access to your home. Ensure that wooden structural elements, siding, or mulching do not touch the soil. Creating a buffer zone can make a significant difference.

Recognizing the Signs: Do You Need Termite Treatment?

Sometimes, the signs of a termite infestation can be subtle, but ignoring them can be costly. Being aware of the common indicators can help you catch a problem early. Many homeowners in East Lansing, including areas around Okemos and Lansing, might not realize they have termites until significant damage has occurred. Our termite treatment in East Lansing, MI, is designed to address these issues head-on.

Common Signs of Termite Activity

Understanding what to look for can make a big difference in protecting your property. Here are some common signs that may indicate the presence of termites:

Mud Tubes

Subterranean termites build mud tubes to travel between their underground colonies and the wood sources they feed on. These tubes, often found on the foundation, walls, or even inside the house, are about the width of a pencil and provide protection from predators and dry environments. If you see these structures, it's a strong indicator of termite activity.

Discovering mud tubes suggests that termites are actively foraging. While they might be visible on the exterior foundation, they can also be found in less obvious places like crawl spaces or basements. Disturbing a mud tube might reveal live termites, confirming the infestation. Ignoring mud tubes allows termites to continue their destructive feeding uninterrupted.

Discarded Wings

Swarming termites, known as swarmers, are winged reproductive termites that leave the colony to establish new ones. After a swarm, they shed their wings, often leaving piles of discarded wings near windowsills, doorways, or light fixtures. These discarded wings are a key indicator of a recent termite swarm near or on your property.

Seeing discarded wings is often one of the first visible signs of a termite infestation. Swarming typically occurs during warmer months, particularly after rain. While the swarm itself might be brief, the presence of wings confirms that a mature colony is nearby and new colonies may be forming. Don't confuse termite wings with ant wings; termite wings are equal in size, while ant wings are unequal.

Damaged or Hollow-Sounding Wood

Termites consume wood from the inside out, often leaving a thin veneer of wood or paint on the surface. Wood infested with termites might sound hollow when tapped. You might also notice blistering or darkening of the wood where termites are active.

Termite damage can be extensive before it becomes outwardly apparent. The hollow sound is a result of the termites eating tunnels within the wood. This damage compromises the structural integrity of the wood and, over time, can weaken the entire structure of your property. Early detection of damaged wood is crucial for limiting the extent of the repairs needed.

Termite Droppings (Frass)

Drywood termites, in particular, produce small, pellet-like droppings called frass. These droppings are often pushed out of small holes near infested wood and may accumulate in small piles below the damaged area. The appearance of these tiny pellets is a strong sign of a drywood termite infestation.

Frass provides another visual clue to termite activity, specifically with drywood termites which do not require contact with soil. The color of the frass can vary depending on the type of wood the termites are consuming. Finding these small, grainy piles is a definite indicator that you need professional termite treatment.

Clicking Sounds in Walls

Some people report hearing faint clicking or rustling sounds within their walls, which can be caused by termites chewing through wood. Soldier termites, when disturbed, may also bang their heads against the wood, creating an audible clicking sound.

While not always easy to detect, hearing sounds from within your walls can be unsettling and a potential sign of a termite problem. This auditory clue suggests an active and possibly significant infestation behind the scenes. If you suspect termites based on sounds, a professional inspection is highly recommended to pinpoint the source.

If you notice any of these signs, it's essential to contact a professional pest control service like East Lansing Pest Pros for a thorough inspection. Delaying action can lead to more extensive and costly damage.

Understanding Termite Species and Their Behavior

In the East Lansing, Michigan area, the most common type of termite is the subterranean termite. Understanding their behavior is key to effective treatment and prevention. Subterranean termites live in large colonies underground and build intricate tunnel systems to access food sources, which are primarily wood and other cellulose-containing materials.

Subterranean Termites

Subterranean termites require moisture to survive. They typically live in the soil and build mud tubes to travel above ground to find food while protecting themselves from drying out. Their colonies can be massive, containing hundreds of thousands or even millions of individuals. They are responsible for the majority of termite damage in the United States.

Their dependence on moisture means that areas with poor drainage, leaky pipes, or excessive humidity are particularly vulnerable to subterranean termite infestations. They consume wood ravenously, and because they do so from within, the damage can go unnoticed for a long time. Identifying their mud tubes is often the first concrete sign of their presence.

Swarming Season

Termite swarming typically occurs in the spring, often after rain. Swarmers are attracted to light and are often found near windows and doors. While the swarm itself is temporary, it is a clear indication that a mature termite colony is nearby and could be infesting your property or a neighbor's. Neighboring areas like Haslett and Williamston may also experience similar swarming events.

Ignoring a termite swarm is similar to ignoring a warning signal. It means there is a well-established colony producing reproductive individuals. Even if the swarm doesn't originate on your property, it signifies a risk, as new colonies can be established nearby. Prompt action after observing a swarm is crucial for preventing or mitigating an infestation.
